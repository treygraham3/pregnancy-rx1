Isotretinoin (Brand name: Accutane)

← Back to home

What is Isotretinoin used for?

Isotretinoin is a powerful medication used to treat severe acne that hasn't responded to other treatments.

Is Isotretinoin safe during pregnancy?

No. Isotretinoin is not safe to take during pregnancy. It is extremely teratogenic and can cause severe birth defects, miscarriage, or fetal death.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category X. Contraindicated in pregnancy. Use is restricted under the iPLEDGE program.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Absolutely contraindicated.

2nd Trimester: Do not use. Severe risks to fetal development.

3rd Trimester: Never permitted.

What are safer alternatives?

Talk to your doctor about switching to safer medications. Options may include:

  • Topical clindamycin
  • Azelaic acid
  • Low-dose benzoyl peroxide

Why is Isotretinoin harmful during pregnancy?

Isotretinoin interferes with fetal cell growth and brain, heart, and facial development. Birth defects occur in more than 25% of pregnancies exposed.

What should I do if I'm taking Isotretinoin and find out I'm pregnant?

Stop isotretinoin immediately and contact your healthcare provider. Pregnancy should be avoided for at least 1 month after stopping.
