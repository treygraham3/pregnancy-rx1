Losartan (Brand name: Cozaar)

← Back to home

What is Losartan used for?

Losartan is used to treat high blood pressure and protect kidney function in people with diabetes. It belongs to a class called ARBs.

Is Losartan safe during pregnancy?

No. Losartan is not safe to take during pregnancy. Like other ARBs, it can harm fetal kidney development, cause low amniotic fluid, and result in poor fetal growth or death.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category D (1st trimester) and X (2nd/3rd). Avoid throughout pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Should be avoided. Risk of early fetal kidney damage.

2nd Trimester: Contraindicated. Can cause fetal kidney failure, low fluid, and poor growth.

3rd Trimester: Strictly avoid.

What are safer alternatives?

Talk to your doctor about switching to safer medications. Options may include:

  • Labetalol
  • Nifedipine
  • Methyldopa

Why is Losartan harmful during pregnancy?

It disrupts a hormone system needed for fetal kidney development and can result in severe complications.

What should I do if I'm taking Losartan and find out I'm pregnant?

Stop immediately under supervision and switch to a safer option.
