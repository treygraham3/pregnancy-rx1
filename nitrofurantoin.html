Nitrofurantoin (Brand names: Macrobid, Macrodantin)

← Back to home

What is Nitrofurantoin used for?

Nitrofurantoin is an antibiotic used to treat urinary tract infections (UTIs). It is especially effective against the bacteria that commonly cause UTIs.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Generally considered safe during most of pregnancy. It may be avoided close to delivery due to a rare risk of infant blood issues.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Was previously Category B. Widely used and considered appropriate when other options are limited.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Can be used but other options may be preferred early in pregnancy.

2nd Trimester: Commonly used and considered safe.

3rd Trimester: May be avoided near term due to risk of hemolytic anemia in newborns.

Current Recommendation

Often used to treat UTIs in the middle part of pregnancy when it's most effective and safest.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Generally safe while breastfeeding. Use caution if baby is premature or has a known enzyme deficiency.

Why might Nitrofurantoin be prescribed during pregnancy?

To treat UTIs, which are common in pregnancy and can lead to complications if untreated.

How does Nitrofurantoin work?

It damages bacterial DNA, killing the bacteria in the urinary tract that are causing infection.

Common Side Effects

  • Nausea
  • Headache
  • Urine discoloration (brown)
  • Rash
