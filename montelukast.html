Montelukast

Used to manage asthma and allergy symptoms

← Back to home
Use During Pregnancy Montelukast is generally considered safe during all trimesters. There is no evidence that it increases the risk of birth defects or pregnancy complications.
Use While Breastfeeding Montelukast appears in breast milk in very low amounts and is considered safe during breastfeeding.
Why It's Used During Pregnancy Montelukast is a leukotriene receptor antagonist used to manage long-term asthma symptoms and allergies. Keeping asthma under control during pregnancy is essential for maternal and fetal health.
Safety Summary This medication is well tolerated and has not been shown to cause harm to the developing baby. It may be continued if already being used before pregnancy or prescribed if asthma symptoms require additional control.
What to Discuss With Your Provider
  • Should I stay on montelukast or switch to a different asthma medication?
  • How can I monitor for effectiveness or side effects during pregnancy?
  • Do I need to continue montelukast after delivery?
