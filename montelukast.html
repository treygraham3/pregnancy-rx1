Montelukast (Brand name: Singulair)

What is Montelukast used for?

Montelukast is a leukotriene receptor antagonist used to manage asthma and allergic rhinitis (hay fever). It helps prevent wheezing and shortness of breath.

Is Montelukast safe during pregnancy?

Yes, with caution. Montelukast is generally considered safe during pregnancy. While data is limited, no increased risk of birth defects has been found in available studies.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category B. Animal studies have not shown harm, and available human data is reassuring.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Can be used if necessary to control asthma or allergies.

2nd Trimester: Continued use is generally safe with provider guidance.

3rd Trimester: Safe when needed to maintain asthma control.

Current Recommendation

Montelukast may be continued if it helps maintain stable asthma control. Poorly controlled asthma poses a higher risk to pregnancy than the medication itself.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Montelukast is considered safe during breastfeeding. Only small amounts pass into breast milk.

Why might Montelukast be prescribed during pregnancy?

To manage asthma or allergy symptoms and maintain normal breathing during pregnancy.

How does Montelukast work?

It blocks leukotrienes, which are chemicals that cause inflammation and tightening of the airways in asthma.

Common Side Effects

  • Headache
  • Fatigue
  • Mood changes (rare)
