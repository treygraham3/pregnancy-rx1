Budesonide (Brand names: Pulmicort, Rhinocort)

← Back to home

What is Budesonide used for?

Budesonide is an inhaled or intranasal corticosteroid used to manage asthma and seasonal allergies. It reduces inflammation in the airways and nasal passages.

Is Budesonide safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Budesonide is the preferred inhaled steroid for asthma during pregnancy and has been studied more extensively than other options.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category B. Clinical studies and experience support its safety and effectiveness during pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe and recommended if asthma is present.

2nd Trimester: Continued use is considered safe and important to reduce asthma risk.

3rd Trimester: Safe and often used to maintain breathing stability.

Current Recommendation

Recommended for asthma and allergic rhinitis when steroid therapy is needed. Helps prevent asthma complications during pregnancy.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Budesonide is safe during breastfeeding. Very little reaches the baby through breast milk.

Why might Budesonide be prescribed during pregnancy?

To control asthma or allergies and reduce the risk of complications such as low oxygen levels, preterm birth, or preeclampsia.

How does Budesonide work?

It reduces inflammation in the lungs and nasal passages, helping to prevent asthma attacks and allergy symptoms.

Common Side Effects

  • Oral thrush (inhaled form)
  • Hoarseness
  • Nose irritation (nasal spray)
