What is Levothyroxine used for?

Levothyroxine is a synthetic form of the thyroid hormone T4. It is used to treat hypothyroidism, or low thyroid hormone levels.

Is Levothyroxine safe during pregnancy?

Yes. It is very important to continue levothyroxine during pregnancy if you have hypothyroidism. Untreated hypothyroidism can lead to complications for both parent and baby.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category A. Levothyroxine is one of the safest and most essential medications for use in pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe and critical for fetal brain and spinal cord development.

2nd Trimester: Safe and often requires dose adjustments.

3rd Trimester: Safe and typically continued through delivery.

Current Recommendation

Continue or begin levothyroxine if prescribed. Regular monitoring of thyroid hormone levels during pregnancy is important.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Levothyroxine is safe during breastfeeding. It does not harm the baby and supports the parent’s thyroid health.

Why might Levothyroxine be prescribed during pregnancy?

To maintain normal thyroid levels and support the baby's development, especially the brain and nervous system.

How does Levothyroxine work?

It replaces or supplements the natural thyroid hormone thyroxine (T4) to regulate the body's metabolism and development processes.

Common Side Effects

  • Symptoms usually occur with under- or over-dosing
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Insomnia
  • Weight changes
