Aspirin (Low-Dose)

← Back to home

What is Low-Dose Aspirin used for?

Low-dose aspirin (81 mg) is used to prevent preeclampsia and improve pregnancy outcomes in people at high risk. It helps support healthy blood flow to the placenta.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Low-dose aspirin is considered safe when prescribed by a provider. It has been shown to reduce the risk of complications like preeclampsia and growth restriction in the baby.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

While high-dose aspirin is not safe during pregnancy, low-dose aspirin has been recommended by major health organizations like ACOG and the USPSTF for certain patients.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe when used under provider guidance.

2nd Trimester: Safe and commonly prescribed for high-risk pregnancies.

3rd Trimester: Often continued unless directed otherwise near delivery.

Current Recommendation

Your provider may recommend low-dose aspirin starting in early pregnancy and continuing until near delivery to reduce risks of high blood pressure-related complications.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Low-dose aspirin is considered safe while breastfeeding. Only minimal amounts pass into breast milk.

Why might Low-Dose Aspirin be prescribed during pregnancy?

To help reduce the risk of preeclampsia, especially in people with prior history, chronic hypertension, or other risk factors.

How does Low-Dose Aspirin work?

It prevents platelets from clumping together, reducing blood clot risk and supporting better blood flow to the baby through the placenta.

Common Side Effects

  • Stomach upset
  • Minor bleeding or bruising
  • Nausea
