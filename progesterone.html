What is Progesterone used for?

Progesterone is a hormone used to support pregnancy in individuals with a history of miscarriage, low hormone levels, or risk of preterm labor.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Progesterone is often used to support early pregnancy or help reduce the risk of preterm birth. It is considered safe when used as prescribed.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Was previously listed under multiple categories depending on formulation. Clinical use has shown it to be safe in appropriate cases.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Commonly used to support early pregnancy. Safe when medically indicated.

2nd Trimester: May be used to prevent preterm labor.

3rd Trimester: Not commonly used but may be continued if started earlier.

Current Recommendation

May be prescribed if hormone support is needed or to prevent early delivery in at-risk pregnancies.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Considered safe while breastfeeding. Naturally present in the body and does not harm the infant.

Why might Progesterone be prescribed during pregnancy?

To support the uterine lining in early pregnancy or prevent premature birth in high-risk individuals.

How does Progesterone work?

It stabilizes the uterine lining and reduces uterine contractions to help support and maintain pregnancy.

Common Side Effects

  • Sleepiness
  • Breast tenderness
  • Mood swings
  • Spotting or cramping
