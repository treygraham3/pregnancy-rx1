Lisinopril (Brand name: Prinivil, Zestril)

What is Lisinopril used for?

Lisinopril is a medication used to treat high blood pressure, heart failure, and to protect the kidneys in people with diabetes. It belongs to a class of drugs called ACE inhibitors.

Is Lisinopril safe during pregnancy?

No. Lisinopril is not safe to take during pregnancy. It can cause serious harm to a developing baby, especially during the second and third trimesters. It may lead to low amniotic fluid levels, poor fetal kidney development, skull abnormalities, and even pregnancy loss.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Lisinopril was classified as Category D in the first trimester (risk exists, but benefits may outweigh risks) and Category X in the second and third trimesters (known to cause fetal harm). It is absolutely contraindicated later in pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Possible risk of harm. Safer medications are preferred.

2nd Trimester: Do not use. Can cause fetal kidney damage and other complications.

3rd Trimester: Strictly avoided. High risk of stillbirth, kidney failure, or skull deformities in the baby.

What are safer alternatives?

Talk to your doctor about switching to safer blood pressure medications. Options may include:

  • Labetalol
  • Nifedipine
  • Methyldopa

Why is Lisinopril harmful during pregnancy?

It interferes with a hormone system that is critical for fetal kidney development and blood flow. This can cause severe complications, including death of the fetus or newborn.

What should I do if I'm taking Lisinopril and find out I'm pregnant?

Contact your healthcare provider right away. Do not stop any medication without guidance, but Lisinopril should be discontinued as soon as it is safely possible.
