Prednisone (Brand names: Deltasone, Rayos)

What is Prednisone used for?

Prednisone is a corticosteroid used to reduce inflammation in a wide range of conditions including asthma, autoimmune diseases, and allergies.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Prednisone may be used during pregnancy when necessary. Some studies suggest a slightly increased risk of cleft lip with early use, but the overall risk is low.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category C. While there are some risks, the benefits often outweigh them in those with moderate to severe inflammation.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Slight increase in cleft lip risk reported; use when benefits outweigh risks.

2nd Trimester: Can be continued under supervision.

3rd Trimester: Generally considered safe. Monitor for maternal blood pressure and blood sugar.

Current Recommendation

If prednisone is needed to manage serious inflammation, it may be continued during pregnancy under medical guidance.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Prednisone is safe while breastfeeding at standard doses. High doses may require short-term timing adjustments for feeding.

Why might Prednisone be prescribed during pregnancy?

To manage flares of autoimmune disease, asthma, or allergic reactions when other options are not sufficient.

How does Prednisone work?

It mimics natural hormones that reduce inflammation and calm overactive immune responses.

Common Side Effects

  • Weight gain
  • Insomnia
  • Elevated blood pressure or blood sugar
  • Mood changes
