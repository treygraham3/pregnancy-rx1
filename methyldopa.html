What is Methyldopa used for?

Methyldopa is an antihypertensive medication used to treat high blood pressure. It has been used for decades in pregnancy.

Is Methyldopa safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Methyldopa is considered one of the safest medications for treating high blood pressure during pregnancy.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category B. It is supported by decades of use and clinical guidelines in pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe and commonly initiated if needed.

2nd Trimester: Continued use is considered safe.

3rd Trimester: Safe when used to manage blood pressure.

Current Recommendation

Methyldopa is a recommended first-line medication for managing chronic hypertension in pregnancy.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Considered safe. Small amounts enter breast milk but are not known to affect the baby.

Why might Methyldopa be prescribed during pregnancy?

To reduce the risk of complications from high blood pressure, such as stroke or preeclampsia.

How does Methyldopa work?

It lowers blood pressure by acting on the central nervous system to relax blood vessels.

Common Side Effects

  • Drowsiness
  • Dry mouth
  • Fatigue
