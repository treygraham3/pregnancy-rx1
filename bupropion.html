Bupropion (Brand names: Wellbutrin, Zyban)

What is Bupropion used for?

Bupropion is an antidepressant used to treat depression and help people stop smoking. It works differently than SSRIs and is often used when other antidepressants aren't effective or cause side effects.

Is Bupropion safe during pregnancy?

Yes, with caution. Bupropion is generally considered safe when the benefits outweigh risks, particularly for depression or smoking cessation. Some studies have suggested a small risk of heart defects, but findings are not conclusive.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category C. Most research suggests it does not significantly increase the risk of birth defects.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Use if clinically necessary. Small risk of heart defects seen in some studies.

2nd Trimester: Can be continued under supervision.

3rd Trimester: Generally safe. May require monitoring for neonatal symptoms after delivery.

Current Recommendation

If you are already using bupropion and it is working well, your provider may recommend continuing it. Do not stop without medical advice.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Use with caution. Small amounts pass into breast milk. Monitor baby for irritability or poor feeding.

Why might Bupropion be prescribed during pregnancy?

To manage depression or help stop smoking—both important for the health of the parent and baby.

How does Bupropion work?

It affects the levels of dopamine and norepinephrine in the brain, improving mood and reducing nicotine cravings.

Common Side Effects

  • Dry mouth
  • Insomnia
  • Increased anxiety (early in treatment)
  • Seizure risk at high doses
