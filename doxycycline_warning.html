What is Doxycycline used for?

Doxycycline is a tetracycline antibiotic used to treat a variety of bacterial infections including acne, respiratory infections, and Lyme disease.

Is Doxycycline safe during pregnancy?

No. Doxycycline is not safe to take during pregnancy. It should not be used during pregnancy. It can permanently discolor developing teeth and affect bone growth in the baby.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category D. Contraindicated during second and third trimesters.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Use only if absolutely necessary.

2nd Trimester: Avoid use. Can affect fetal bone and tooth development.

3rd Trimester: Contraindicated.

What are safer alternatives?

Talk to your doctor about switching to safer medications. Options may include:

  • Amoxicillin
  • Azithromycin

Why is Doxycycline harmful during pregnancy?

Doxycycline binds to calcium and can accumulate in fetal bones and teeth, leading to staining and skeletal development problems.

What should I do if I'm taking Doxycycline and find out I'm pregnant?

Stop and switch to a safer antibiotic in consultation with your provider.
