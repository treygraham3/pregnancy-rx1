Xofluza (Baloxavir Marboxil)

← Back to home

What is Xofluza used for?

Xofluza is a single-dose antiviral medication used to treat influenza (the flu) in people aged 12 and older. It is effective when taken within 48 hours of symptoms starting.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

There is limited data on Xofluza use during pregnancy. It is not the first choice for pregnant people unless no other treatment is available or Tamiflu cannot be used.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Xofluza is not assigned a pregnancy category. Limited human studies are available, so it is only used when clearly needed.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Not recommended due to limited safety data.

2nd Trimester: Use only if Tamiflu is not an option.

3rd Trimester: Avoid unless no alternatives are available.

Current Recommendation

Xofluza is typically avoided in pregnancy. It may be considered in specific cases where other antivirals are not appropriate.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

There is not enough data to determine Xofluza’s safety during breastfeeding. Other medications are preferred.

Why might Xofluza be prescribed during pregnancy?

In rare cases, when Tamiflu is not suitable and rapid flu treatment is needed, a provider may consider Xofluza.

How does Xofluza work?

It inhibits the flu virus’s ability to replicate by blocking the polymerase enzyme involved in copying viral genetic material.

Common Side Effects

  • Diarrhea
  • Headache
  • Nausea
