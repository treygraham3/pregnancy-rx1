Ondansetron (Brand name: Zofran)

← Back to home

What is Ondansetron used for?

Ondansetron is used to prevent or treat nausea and vomiting. It is commonly prescribed for people experiencing morning sickness during pregnancy.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Studies on ondansetron have shown mixed results. Most recent studies suggest it does not significantly increase the risk of birth defects, but it should be used only if other options have not worked.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Ondansetron was previously categorized as Category B. It is now assessed case-by-case under FDA narrative labeling. Use during early pregnancy should be discussed with a healthcare provider.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Some studies show small increased risk of cleft palate. Use when benefits outweigh risks.

2nd Trimester: Generally considered safe if already in use.

3rd Trimester: Can be continued if needed. Monitor for constipation or QT prolongation.

Current Recommendation

May be prescribed if symptoms are severe and not responding to other treatments. Use under provider supervision.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Ondansetron is considered safe while breastfeeding. Very small amounts appear in breast milk.

Why might Ondansetron be prescribed during pregnancy?

To help manage moderate to severe nausea and vomiting (hyperemesis gravidarum) when dietary and lifestyle changes or other medications have not helped.

How does Ondansetron work?

It blocks serotonin receptors in the brain and gut that trigger nausea and vomiting.

Common Side Effects

  • Constipation
  • Headache
  • Drowsiness
  • Rare heart rhythm changes (QT prolongation)
