Lorazepam (Brand name: Ativan)

What is Lorazepam used for?

Lorazepam is a benzodiazepine used to treat anxiety, seizures, and insomnia.

Is Lorazepam safe during pregnancy?

No. Lorazepam is not safe to take during pregnancy. It should generally be avoided during pregnancy. It can cause sedation in the fetus and withdrawal symptoms in newborns.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category D. May be used in rare cases when the benefits outweigh the risks.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Linked to increased risk of cleft palate.

2nd Trimester: Use only with caution.

3rd Trimester: Avoid use. Risk of sedation and withdrawal in newborns.

What are safer alternatives?

Talk to your doctor about switching to safer medications. Options may include:

  • Hydroxyzine
  • Buspirone
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Why is Lorazepam harmful during pregnancy?

Benzodiazepines cross the placenta and can affect fetal brain development, cause drowsiness, and lead to withdrawal symptoms in the newborn.

What should I do if I'm taking Lorazepam and find out I'm pregnant?

Contact your doctor to taper and transition to a safer treatment.
