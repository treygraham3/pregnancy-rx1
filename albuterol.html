Albuterol (Brand names: ProAir, Ventolin, Proventil)

What is Albuterol used for?

Albuterol is a short-acting beta agonist (SABA) used to relieve symptoms of asthma, such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath. It is typically used as a rescue inhaler during asthma attacks or before exercise.

Is Albuterol safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Albuterol is considered safe to use during pregnancy. Proper asthma control is important for the health of both the parent and the baby.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Albuterol was previously listed as Category C. However, studies and clinical experience have shown it to be safe and effective for managing asthma during pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe to use as needed to maintain good asthma control.

2nd Trimester: Continued use is safe. Ensures good oxygenation for the baby.

3rd Trimester: Safe when needed. May be used up until delivery under provider guidance.

Current Recommendation

Continue using albuterol as prescribed. Do not stop or reduce asthma medications without discussing with your provider. Uncontrolled asthma poses a higher risk than the medication itself.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Albuterol is safe while breastfeeding. Very little of the medication passes into breast milk and is unlikely to affect the baby.

Why might Albuterol be prescribed during pregnancy?

To treat or prevent asthma attacks and ensure proper oxygen delivery to both the pregnant individual and the baby.

How does Albuterol work?

It relaxes the muscles around the airways, allowing them to open and make breathing easier during asthma symptoms or attacks.

Common Side Effects

  • Shakiness
  • Increased heart rate
  • Nervousness
  • Headache
