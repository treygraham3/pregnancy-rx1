What is Insulin used for?

Insulin is a hormone that lowers blood sugar levels. It is used to manage type 1 diabetes, type 2 diabetes, and gestational diabetes.

Is Insulin safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Insulin is the preferred treatment for managing blood sugar during pregnancy. It does not cross the placenta and is safe for the baby.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Insulin has long been considered safe for use in pregnancy and is supported by numerous clinical guidelines.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe and often started early in diabetes management.

2nd Trimester: Safe and commonly continued. Dose may be adjusted as pregnancy progresses.

3rd Trimester: Safe and essential to prevent complications like large birth weight or preterm delivery.

Current Recommendation

Insulin is recommended when blood sugar cannot be controlled through diet and exercise alone. It's essential for keeping both parent and baby healthy.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Insulin is safe during breastfeeding. It does not affect milk supply or pass into breast milk in meaningful amounts.

Why might Insulin be prescribed during pregnancy?

To manage blood sugar and prevent complications like preeclampsia, miscarriage, or birth defects due to uncontrolled diabetes.

How does Insulin work?

It helps move sugar from the bloodstream into the cells where it can be used for energy.

Common Side Effects

  • Low blood sugar (hypoglycemia)
  • Weight gain
  • Injection site irritation
