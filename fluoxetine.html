Fluoxetine (Brand name: Prozac)

← Back to home

What is Fluoxetine used for?

Fluoxetine is an SSRI antidepressant used to treat depression, anxiety, OCD, and other mood disorders.

Is Fluoxetine safe during pregnancy?

Yes, with monitoring. Fluoxetine is one of the more well-studied SSRIs during pregnancy. While it may carry a slightly higher risk of neonatal complications than other SSRIs, it is generally considered safe when benefits outweigh risks.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category C. Most data support its safety, though some studies suggest a small increase in the risk of birth defects or neonatal adaptation syndrome.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: May be used if needed. Small increased risk of congenital heart defects has been noted in some studies.

2nd Trimester: Often continued with provider monitoring.

3rd Trimester: Continue with caution. Monitor newborn for withdrawal or breathing issues.

Current Recommendation

Fluoxetine may be used throughout pregnancy if necessary for mental health. Do not stop abruptly without provider guidance.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Generally safe while breastfeeding, although other SSRIs may be preferred. Monitor baby for sleep or feeding changes.

Why might Fluoxetine be prescribed during pregnancy?

To manage depression, anxiety, or obsessive-compulsive disorder during pregnancy to support overall health.

How does Fluoxetine work?

It increases serotonin levels in the brain, which helps regulate mood and emotional stability.

Common Side Effects

  • Headache
  • Nausea
  • Insomnia
  • Sexual side effects
