Jardiance (Generic name: Empagliflozin)

← Back to home

What is Jardiance used for?

Jardiance is a medication used to lower blood sugar in people with type 2 diabetes. It is also used to reduce the risk of heart failure hospitalization in people with cardiovascular disease.

Is Jardiance safe during pregnancy?

No. Jardiance is not recommended during pregnancy. Animal studies suggest it may affect kidney development in the fetus, particularly in the second and third trimesters.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Not assigned a pregnancy category under the new FDA labeling rules. Clinical guidelines advise against use during pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Avoid if possible. Safety data is limited.

2nd Trimester: Contraindicated. Risk of kidney development issues.

3rd Trimester: Contraindicated. Can impair fetal kidney function.

Current Recommendation

If you are taking Jardiance and become pregnant, contact your healthcare provider immediately. It will likely be stopped and replaced with a safer option.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Not recommended. It is unknown if Jardiance passes into breast milk, but due to its mechanism, it could affect a nursing infant.

Why might Jardiance be prescribed during pregnancy?

It typically is not. Safer medications are preferred for managing diabetes during pregnancy.

How does Jardiance work?

It lowers blood sugar by helping the kidneys remove glucose through the urine. This mechanism may affect fetal kidney development.

Common Side Effects

  • Urinary tract infections
  • Yeast infections
  • Dehydration
  • Increased urination
