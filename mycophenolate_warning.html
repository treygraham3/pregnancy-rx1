What is Mycophenolate used for?

Mycophenolate is an immunosuppressive drug used to prevent organ rejection and treat autoimmune diseases.

Is Mycophenolate safe during pregnancy?

No. Mycophenolate is not safe to take during pregnancy. It is highly teratogenic and should never be used during pregnancy. It increases the risk of miscarriage and severe birth defects.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category D. Contraindicated unless no alternatives are available.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Strongly contraindicated.

2nd Trimester: Do not use.

3rd Trimester: Unsafe for use.

What are safer alternatives?

Talk to your doctor about switching to safer medications. Options may include:

  • Azathioprine
  • Tacrolimus

Why is Mycophenolate harmful during pregnancy?

Mycophenolate disrupts cell growth and DNA replication, leading to physical malformations and brain development problems.

What should I do if I'm taking Mycophenolate and find out I'm pregnant?

Discontinue immediately and consult a specialist for alternative treatments.
