Hydroxychloroquine (Brand name: Plaquenil)

← Back to home

What is Hydroxychloroquine used for?

Hydroxychloroquine is used to treat autoimmune diseases such as lupus and rheumatoid arthritis. It reduces inflammation and helps prevent disease flare-ups.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Hydroxychloroquine is considered safe for use during pregnancy. It has not been shown to increase the risk of birth defects or pregnancy complications.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously classified as Category C, but more recent studies support its safety when used during pregnancy, especially when needed to control autoimmune conditions.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: No increased risk of birth defects. Continued use is generally safe.

2nd Trimester: Can be continued under supervision to maintain disease control.

3rd Trimester: Safe to continue. Stopping may lead to flare-ups.

Current Recommendation

Your doctor may recommend continuing this medication during pregnancy if it helps control symptoms and reduces disease-related risks.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Hydroxychloroquine is considered safe while breastfeeding. Only low levels appear in breast milk.

Why might Hydroxychloroquine be prescribed during pregnancy?

Managing autoimmune diseases is essential for pregnancy success. Flares of lupus or arthritis can pose greater risks than continuing hydroxychloroquine.

How does Hydroxychloroquine work?

It modifies immune system activity, reducing inflammation and preventing immune attacks on the body’s own tissues.

Common Side Effects

  • Nausea
  • Skin rash
  • Vision changes (rare with long-term use)
  • Fatigue
