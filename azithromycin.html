Azithromycin (Brand name: Z-Pak)

← Back to home

What is Azithromycin used for?

Azithromycin is an antibiotic used to treat respiratory infections, sexually transmitted infections, and skin infections.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Azithromycin is considered safe during pregnancy. Studies have not shown increased risk of birth defects or pregnancy complications.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously classified as Category B. It is frequently used in pregnancy when antibiotics are needed.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Considered safe based on current data.

2nd Trimester: Safe and effective for common infections.

3rd Trimester: Safe under supervision for infections near delivery.

Current Recommendation

Often used when penicillin-type antibiotics cannot be taken or if specific infections call for it.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Azithromycin is considered safe while breastfeeding. Only small amounts are present in breast milk.

Why might Azithromycin be prescribed during pregnancy?

To treat infections like chlamydia, bronchitis, or pneumonia that could affect pregnancy health if untreated.

How does Azithromycin work?

It stops bacteria from making proteins they need to grow and multiply, allowing the immune system to clear the infection.

Common Side Effects

  • Upset stomach
  • Diarrhea
  • Nausea
  • Headache
