What is Amoxicillin used for?

Amoxicillin is a common antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections such as urinary tract infections, ear infections, strep throat, and sinusitis.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Amoxicillin is considered very safe to use during pregnancy. It has been used for decades and is not linked to birth defects or pregnancy complications.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Amoxicillin was previously classified as Category B. It is widely used in pregnancy and supported by research as a safe antibiotic choice.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe for use. No increased risk of birth defects reported.

2nd Trimester: Safe and commonly prescribed.

3rd Trimester: Safe when needed for infection control.

Current Recommendation

Amoxicillin may be used during pregnancy to treat infections when prescribed by your provider. Prompt treatment of infections is important for a healthy pregnancy.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Amoxicillin is considered safe while breastfeeding. It passes into breast milk in very small amounts and is well tolerated by infants.

Why might Amoxicillin be prescribed during pregnancy?

To treat bacterial infections such as UTIs, respiratory infections, and dental abscesses, which can lead to complications if not treated promptly.

How does Amoxicillin work?

It kills bacteria by interfering with their ability to build a protective cell wall, helping your body fight off infection.

Common Side Effects

  • Upset stomach
  • Diarrhea
  • Rash
  • Yeast infection
