Atorvastatin (Brand name: Lipitor)

What is Atorvastatin used for?

Atorvastatin is a statin used to lower cholesterol and reduce the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Is Atorvastatin safe during pregnancy?

No. Atorvastatin is not safe to take during pregnancy. It is not safe to use during pregnancy. Cholesterol is essential for fetal development, and statins may interfere with that process.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category X. Statins are contraindicated in pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Avoid use. May interfere with organ and nervous system development.

2nd Trimester: Not safe.

3rd Trimester: Contraindicated.

What are safer alternatives?

Talk to your doctor about switching to safer medications. Options may include:

  • Diet and lifestyle modifications
  • Bile acid sequestrants (rare cases)

Why is Atorvastatin harmful during pregnancy?

Statins inhibit cholesterol synthesis, which is essential for cell membrane formation and fetal development.

What should I do if I'm taking Atorvastatin and find out I'm pregnant?

Stop atorvastatin immediately and consult your doctor for a safer alternative.
