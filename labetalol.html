Labetalol (Brand name: Trandate)

What is Labetalol used for?

Labetalol is a medication used to treat high blood pressure. It is often prescribed during pregnancy to manage chronic hypertension or preeclampsia.

Is Labetalol safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Labetalol is considered one of the safest and most commonly used blood pressure medications in pregnancy.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Labetalol was previously categorized as Category C, but current data and guidelines support its use in pregnancy, especially when blood pressure control is necessary.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe when needed. Often started early to manage chronic hypertension.

2nd Trimester: Commonly used and well tolerated.

3rd Trimester: Continued use is safe. May be adjusted close to delivery depending on maternal blood pressure.

Current Recommendation

Recommended for people with chronic high blood pressure or preeclampsia. Helps prevent complications like stroke, preterm delivery, or poor fetal growth.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Labetalol is considered safe while breastfeeding. Only small amounts pass into breast milk and are not harmful to the baby.

Why might Labetalol be prescribed during pregnancy?

To control high blood pressure and reduce the risk of pregnancy complications such as placental abruption, preterm labor, and stroke.

How does Labetalol work?

It blocks both alpha and beta receptors, helping to lower blood pressure by relaxing blood vessels and slowing the heart rate.

Common Side Effects

  • Dizziness
  • Tiredness
  • Nausea
  • Cold hands or feet
