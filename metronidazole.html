Metronidazole (Brand names: Flagyl, Metrogel)

← Back to home

What is Metronidazole used for?

Metronidazole is an antibiotic used to treat bacterial vaginosis and other infections caused by anaerobic bacteria or parasites.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Metronidazole is generally considered safe to use during pregnancy. Studies have not shown an increased risk of birth defects.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously categorized as Category B. It has been widely studied and is used safely in pregnancy when prescribed.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: May be used when needed. Most evidence supports its safety.

2nd Trimester: Safe for treating infections like bacterial vaginosis or trichomoniasis.

3rd Trimester: Safe when used appropriately for bacterial infections.

Current Recommendation

Your provider may prescribe metronidazole during pregnancy when an infection needs treatment to prevent complications.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Generally considered safe while breastfeeding. Use with caution for high doses or long-term use.

Why might Metronidazole be prescribed during pregnancy?

To treat bacterial infections that could increase the risk of preterm birth or other complications if left untreated.

How does Metronidazole work?

It disrupts DNA in certain bacteria and parasites, helping to eliminate infection.

Common Side Effects

  • Metallic taste
  • Nausea
  • Loss of appetite
  • Dark urine
