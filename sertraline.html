Is Sertraline Safe During Pregnancy?

  • 1st Trimester: Low to moderate risk
  • 2nd Trimester: Generally considered safe
  • 3rd Trimester: May cause mild withdrawal symptoms in newborns

Is Sertraline Safe While Breastfeeding?

Yes. Sertraline is one of the preferred antidepressants for breastfeeding individuals. Only small amounts pass into breast milk and it is considered safe by most experts.

Why Might Sertraline Be Used During Pregnancy?

Sertraline is an SSRI antidepressant used to treat depression, anxiety, and obsessive-compulsive disorder. Treating mental health conditions during pregnancy is important for both maternal and fetal health. Untreated depression can lead to complications such as poor prenatal care, low birth weight, and postpartum depression.

What Should I Ask My Provider?

  • Is it safe for me to continue sertraline during pregnancy?
  • What are the risks of switching medications during pregnancy?
  • Will my baby need monitoring after birth if I take sertraline in the third trimester?
