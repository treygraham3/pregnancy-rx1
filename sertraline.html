Sertraline (Brand name: Zoloft)

What is Sertraline used for?

Sertraline is an antidepressant in the selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) class. It is used to treat depression, anxiety, PTSD, and OCD.

Is Sertraline safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Sertraline is one of the most commonly prescribed antidepressants during pregnancy. It is generally considered safe, especially when the benefits outweigh potential risks.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category C. Most data support its use in pregnancy with careful monitoring. Untreated depression also carries risks for both parent and baby.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: May be associated with small risk of heart defects. Still commonly used when clinically necessary.

2nd Trimester: Safe when continued under medical guidance.

3rd Trimester: May cause mild neonatal adaptation symptoms but generally safe.

Current Recommendation

Continue use if depression or anxiety symptoms are significant. Mental health is a key part of maternal and fetal well-being.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Sertraline is considered the safest SSRI during breastfeeding. Very low levels transfer into breast milk.

Why might Sertraline be prescribed during pregnancy?

To manage depression, anxiety, or other mood disorders that could negatively affect both parent and baby.

How does Sertraline work?

It increases levels of serotonin in the brain, improving mood and emotional stability.

Common Side Effects

  • Nausea
  • Fatigue
  • Dry mouth
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
