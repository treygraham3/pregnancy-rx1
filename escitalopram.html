Escitalopram (Brand name: Lexapro)

What is Escitalopram used for?

Escitalopram is an SSRI antidepressant used to treat depression and generalized anxiety disorder.

Is Escitalopram safe during pregnancy?

Yes, with caution. Escitalopram is generally considered safe during pregnancy, especially if the benefits outweigh the risks of untreated depression or anxiety.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category C. Most studies do not show major birth defect risks, but newborns may have temporary symptoms after delivery.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Can be used if needed. Data does not show a strong link to major malformations.

2nd Trimester: Often continued. Safe when monitored by a healthcare provider.

3rd Trimester: May cause temporary symptoms in newborns. Typically safe when continued under supervision.

Current Recommendation

Escitalopram is used during pregnancy when mental health treatment is essential. Benefits generally outweigh the risks.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Escitalopram is considered compatible with breastfeeding. Monitor baby for sleep or feeding issues.

Why might Escitalopram be prescribed during pregnancy?

To treat mood disorders that could negatively affect both parent and baby if left untreated.

How does Escitalopram work?

It increases serotonin levels in the brain to improve mood and reduce anxiety symptoms.

Common Side Effects

  • Nausea
  • Fatigue
  • Sexual dysfunction
  • Dizziness
