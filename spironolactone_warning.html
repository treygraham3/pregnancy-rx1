Spironolactone (Brand name: Aldactone)

What is Spironolactone used for?

Spironolactone is used to treat heart failure, high blood pressure, and conditions involving excess fluid retention. It also has anti-androgen effects and is sometimes used off-label for acne or hormonal imbalance.

Is Spironolactone safe during pregnancy?

No. Spironolactone is not safe to take during pregnancy. It is not safe for use during pregnancy due to its potential to interfere with fetal hormone development, especially in male fetuses.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category C. While not extensively studied in pregnancy, known hormonal effects make it contraindicated.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Avoid use. Risk of disrupting fetal sex development.

2nd Trimester: Contraindicated. Anti-androgenic effects can feminize male fetuses.

3rd Trimester: Unsafe for use.

What are safer alternatives?

Talk to your doctor about switching to safer medications. Options may include:

  • Hydrochlorothiazide (with monitoring)
  • Labetalol
  • Nifedipine

Why is Spironolactone harmful during pregnancy?

Spironolactone acts as an androgen receptor blocker, which may interfere with the development of male external genitalia in the fetus.

What should I do if I'm taking Spironolactone and find out I'm pregnant?

Stop spironolactone as soon as pregnancy is confirmed and consult with your doctor to find safer alternatives.
