What is Metformin used for?

Metformin is an oral medication commonly used to manage type 2 diabetes and insulin resistance. It is also used to treat polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).

Is Metformin safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Metformin is considered safe during pregnancy and is often used to manage gestational diabetes or PCOS-related insulin resistance.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category B. Current data support its use in pregnancy, particularly for those who cannot tolerate insulin or need oral therapy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe. Commonly continued if already in use for PCOS or type 2 diabetes.

2nd Trimester: Safe for managing gestational or preexisting diabetes.

3rd Trimester: Safe. May be used alone or in combination with insulin.

Current Recommendation

Metformin is a safe and effective option for managing blood glucose during pregnancy. Your provider may continue or start this medication as needed.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Metformin is safe during breastfeeding. Minimal amounts pass into breast milk and it is not expected to cause harm to the infant.

Why might Metformin be prescribed during pregnancy?

To manage high blood sugar levels in gestational diabetes or preexisting type 2 diabetes, or to support hormonal regulation in PCOS.

How does Metformin work?

It decreases glucose production in the liver and improves insulin sensitivity in the body.

Common Side Effects

  • Nausea
  • Diarrhea
  • Metallic taste
  • Stomach discomfort
