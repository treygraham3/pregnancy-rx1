Nifedipine (Brand names: Procardia, Adalat)

← Back to home

What is Nifedipine used for?

Nifedipine is a calcium channel blocker used to treat high blood pressure and control uterine contractions in preterm labor.

Is Nifedipine safe during pregnancy?

Yes. Nifedipine is considered safe and is commonly used to manage hypertension and preterm contractions in pregnancy.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category C. Current clinical guidelines support its use during pregnancy when indicated.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: May be used if clinically indicated for blood pressure control.

2nd Trimester: Commonly used and considered safe.

3rd Trimester: Safe and often used for blood pressure or as a tocolytic to stop preterm contractions.

Current Recommendation

Recommended for pregnant people who need blood pressure management or treatment of preterm labor.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Nifedipine is considered safe while breastfeeding. Minimal amounts transfer into breast milk.

Why might Nifedipine be prescribed during pregnancy?

To treat high blood pressure or to relax the uterus during premature contractions (tocolysis).

How does Nifedipine work?

It relaxes blood vessels and smooth muscle, lowering blood pressure and reducing uterine activity.

Common Side Effects

  • Headache
  • Flushing
  • Swelling
  • Dizziness
