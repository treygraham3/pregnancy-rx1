Valproic Acid (Brand names: Depakote, Valproate)

← Back to home

What is Valproic Acid used for?

Valproic acid is used to treat epilepsy, bipolar disorder, and migraine prevention.

Is Valproic Acid safe during pregnancy?

No. Valproic Acid is not safe to take during pregnancy. It significantly increases the risk of major birth defects and developmental disorders, especially neural tube defects.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category D. Known to be highly teratogenic and only used if absolutely necessary.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Contraindicated. Causes neural tube defects and other major birth defects.

2nd Trimester: Avoid use. Risk of cognitive and behavioral issues.

3rd Trimester: Continue avoidance. Long-term effects on the baby's brain development.

What are safer alternatives?

Talk to your doctor about switching to safer medications. Options may include:

  • Lamotrigine
  • Levetiracetam

Why is Valproic Acid harmful during pregnancy?

Valproic acid affects the way cells divide and grow, especially in the brain and spinal cord. It is known to cause spina bifida and lower IQ scores.

What should I do if I'm taking Valproic Acid and find out I'm pregnant?

Speak to your doctor immediately. Do not stop seizure medications without medical advice, but an alternative should be found quickly.
