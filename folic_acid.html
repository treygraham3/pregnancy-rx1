What is Folic Acid used for?

Folic acid is a type of B vitamin used to prevent birth defects of the brain and spine. It is recommended for all people who are pregnant or trying to become pregnant.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Folic acid is extremely safe and recommended for all pregnancies. It helps prevent serious birth defects when taken before conception and during early pregnancy.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Folic acid is not assigned a pregnancy risk category because it is a recommended supplement. It is essential to healthy fetal development.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Most critical time for use. Helps prevent neural tube defects.

2nd Trimester: Continued use is safe and beneficial.

3rd Trimester: Safe throughout pregnancy and while breastfeeding.

Current Recommendation

Begin taking at least 400 mcg of folic acid daily before conception and continue through pregnancy. Higher doses may be needed in certain individuals.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Folic acid is safe while breastfeeding. It is a normal part of breast milk and beneficial to both parent and infant.

Why might Folic Acid be prescribed during pregnancy?

To help prevent neural tube defects like spina bifida and support normal fetal growth and development.

How does Folic Acid work?

It helps build DNA and support cell growth, which is especially important in early fetal development.

Common Side Effects

  • Very well tolerated
  • Rare: Nausea, bloating if taken in high doses
