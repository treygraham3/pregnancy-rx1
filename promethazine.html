Promethazine (Brand name: Phenergan)

← Back to home

What is Promethazine used for?

Promethazine is used to treat nausea, vomiting, and motion sickness. It may also help with allergic reactions or trouble sleeping.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Promethazine has been used for many years during pregnancy and is considered generally safe, especially after the first trimester.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Was previously Category C. Newer data suggests it may be safe when used short-term and under medical supervision.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Use with caution. Limited data suggests low risk.

2nd Trimester: Often used for nausea. Generally safe for short-term use.

3rd Trimester: Safe but may cause drowsiness or sedation in the baby if used near delivery.

Current Recommendation

Use for nausea when other treatments are not effective, especially after the first trimester.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Use caution. It can pass into breast milk and cause sedation in infants, especially newborns. Monitor infant closely if used.

Why might Promethazine be prescribed during pregnancy?

To manage nausea and vomiting when symptoms interfere with daily life and non-drug treatments haven’t helped.

How does Promethazine work?

It blocks histamine and dopamine receptors involved in nausea signaling and motion sickness.

Common Side Effects

  • Drowsiness
  • Dizziness
  • Dry mouth
  • Constipation
