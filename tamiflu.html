Tamiflu (Oseltamivir)

← Back to home

What is Tamiflu used for?

Tamiflu is an antiviral medication used to treat and prevent influenza (the flu). It is most effective when started within 48 hours of symptom onset.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Tamiflu is considered safe to use during pregnancy. It is commonly recommended to reduce flu-related complications, which can be more severe in pregnant people.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Tamiflu was previously listed as Category C. However, current recommendations from the CDC support its use in pregnancy due to the higher risk of flu complications.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Considered safe based on available evidence.

2nd Trimester: Safe and often used during flu outbreaks.

3rd Trimester: Safe and recommended when flu exposure or symptoms are present.

Current Recommendation

If you are pregnant and have the flu or were exposed to it, your provider may recommend Tamiflu to reduce the risk of severe illness.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Tamiflu is considered safe during breastfeeding. Only small amounts enter breast milk and it is well tolerated by infants.

Why might Tamiflu be prescribed during pregnancy?

To prevent or treat the flu and reduce the risk of complications such as pneumonia or hospitalization.

How does Tamiflu work?

It blocks an enzyme called neuraminidase that flu viruses use to spread within the body.

Common Side Effects

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Headache
