Enoxaparin (Brand name: Lovenox)

What is Enoxaparin used for?

Enoxaparin is a type of blood thinner (anticoagulant) used to prevent or treat blood clots. It is often prescribed during pregnancy for people with clotting disorders or previous pregnancy complications related to clotting.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Enoxaparin is considered safe during pregnancy and is commonly used to prevent serious complications. It does not cross the placenta and does not affect the baby directly.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Enoxaparin was previously classified as Category B. It has been well studied and is generally recommended for patients at risk of clotting during pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe and commonly started if indicated.

2nd Trimester: Continued use is safe and helps prevent complications.

3rd Trimester: May require dose adjustment before delivery to reduce bleeding risk.

Current Recommendation

If you are at risk for clots, your provider may recommend continuing enoxaparin throughout pregnancy and possibly postpartum.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Enoxaparin does not enter breast milk and is considered safe while breastfeeding.

Why might Enoxaparin be prescribed during pregnancy?

To prevent blood clots in individuals with conditions like antiphospholipid syndrome, thrombophilia, or a history of miscarriage or stillbirth due to clotting issues.

How does Enoxaparin work?

It slows the formation of blood clots by interfering with the body’s clotting factors, helping maintain healthy blood flow during pregnancy.

Common Side Effects

  • Bruising at injection site
  • Mild bleeding
  • Injection site pain
  • Rare allergic reaction
