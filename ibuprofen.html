Ibuprofen (Brand names: Advil, Motrin)

← Back to home

What is Ibuprofen used for?

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to reduce fever, pain, and inflammation. It is commonly available over the counter.

Is Ibuprofen safe during pregnancy?

Use with caution. Ibuprofen is generally avoided in pregnancy, especially during the third trimester. It may increase the risk of complications such as low amniotic fluid or premature closure of the fetal heart duct (ductus arteriosus).

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category C in the first and second trimester, and Category D in the third. The FDA now recommends avoiding use unless absolutely necessary, especially late in pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Use only if benefits outweigh risks. May increase risk of miscarriage if taken frequently.

2nd Trimester: Can be used short-term with provider approval. Avoid prolonged or high-dose use.

3rd Trimester: Avoid use. Can cause fetal heart and kidney complications.

Current Recommendation

Acetaminophen (Tylenol) is generally preferred for pain or fever during pregnancy. Ibuprofen should only be used if advised by your healthcare provider.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Ibuprofen is safe to use while breastfeeding. Only small amounts pass into breast milk.

Why might Ibuprofen be used during pregnancy?

In some cases of acute pain or inflammation where acetaminophen is not effective, short-term use of ibuprofen may be considered early in pregnancy.

How does Ibuprofen work?

It blocks enzymes (COX-1 and COX-2) involved in the production of substances that cause pain and inflammation in the body.

Common Side Effects

  • Stomach upset
  • Heartburn
  • Headache
  • Increased blood pressure (with long-term use)
