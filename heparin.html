What is Heparin used for?

Heparin is a blood thinner used to prevent and treat blood clots. It is often used during pregnancy in individuals with clotting disorders or at high risk for clotting.

Pregnancy Safety Summary

Heparin is considered safe during pregnancy. It does not cross the placenta and has no known harmful effects on the fetus.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Heparin was previously classified as Category C. However, it is now widely accepted as a safe and effective option in pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Safe for those needing clot prevention.

2nd Trimester: Continued use is common for at-risk individuals.

3rd Trimester: May be adjusted or switched before delivery.

Current Recommendation

Used throughout pregnancy to reduce clotting risk in those with a history of thrombosis or clotting disorders.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

Heparin is safe during breastfeeding and does not pass into breast milk.

Why might Heparin be prescribed during pregnancy?

To prevent serious complications from blood clots, especially in people with clotting disorders like antiphospholipid syndrome.

How does Heparin work?

It blocks the formation of blood clots by inactivating clotting factors in the blood.

Common Side Effects

  • Bruising at injection site
  • Mild bleeding
  • Rare allergic reaction
