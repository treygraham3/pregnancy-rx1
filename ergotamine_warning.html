What is Ergotamine used for?

Ergotamine is used to treat migraines. It narrows blood vessels in the brain to relieve headache symptoms.

Is Ergotamine safe during pregnancy?

No. Ergotamine is not safe to take during pregnancy. It should not be used during pregnancy. It can restrict blood flow to the uterus and harm the developing baby.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category X. Absolutely contraindicated in pregnancy.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Contraindicated. May cause miscarriage.

2nd Trimester: Do not use.

3rd Trimester: Strictly avoided.

What are safer alternatives?

Talk to your doctor about switching to safer medications. Options may include:

  • Acetaminophen
  • Sumatriptan

Why is Ergotamine harmful during pregnancy?

Ergotamine causes uterine constriction and reduced placental blood flow, which can lead to miscarriage or fetal distress.

What should I do if I'm taking Ergotamine and find out I'm pregnant?

Stop use immediately and switch to a pregnancy-safe migraine treatment.
