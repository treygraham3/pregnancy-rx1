Citalopram (Brand name: Celexa)

What is Citalopram used for?

Citalopram is an SSRI antidepressant used to treat depression and anxiety disorders.

Is Citalopram safe during pregnancy?

Yes, with monitoring. It is commonly used during pregnancy when the benefits outweigh potential risks. May cause mild withdrawal symptoms in newborns.

FDA Pregnancy Risk Explanation

Previously Category C. Generally considered safe with caution, especially in the third trimester.

Safety by Trimester

1st Trimester: Can be used if needed. Slight association with heart defects seen in some studies.

2nd Trimester: Safe with regular monitoring.

3rd Trimester: May be continued. Monitor for neonatal adaptation syndrome.

Current Recommendation

Citalopram may be continued if depression is well controlled and benefits outweigh the risks. Consult your provider.

Breastfeeding Safety Summary

May be used while breastfeeding. Monitor infant for sedation or irritability.

Why might Citalopram be prescribed during pregnancy?

To help manage depression or anxiety during pregnancy, which is important for maternal and fetal health.

How does Citalopram work?

It increases levels of serotonin in the brain, helping to improve mood and reduce anxiety.

Common Side Effects

  • Nausea
  • Drowsiness
  • Dry mouth
  • Weight changes
